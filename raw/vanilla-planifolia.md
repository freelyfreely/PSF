# Vanilla planifolia — raw

**Identity:** vanilla. Orchidaceae — the only orchid the world eats. PSF role: understory vine
(epiphyte), spice. Copy entry #53.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: pref 2,000–2,500 mm, tol 1,500–3,000 mm → **79–98 in pref, 59–118 in tol**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: pref 21–30 °C, tol 10–33 °C → **70–86 °F pref, 50–91 °F tol**. No frost.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: sea level–600 m → **0–2,000 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: epiphytic, roots into bark and humus pockets, not deep soil; pH pref 5.5–7,
  tol 4.3–8. Deep leaf-litter mulch at the base.
- **Light** — Ferns: semi-shade, wind-protected. Needs a living post to climb.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: cuttings 90–120 cm (≥3 nodes) or 1.5–2 m lengths. Short cuttings (30 cm) fruit 3–5 yr;
  long cuttings (90–100 cm) fruit 1–2 yr. Seed needs fungal symbiosis (not used by growers).
- **Pollination** — Ferns: a specific nocturnal moth/bee, absent outside the native range; **hand-
  pollination required here**, flower by flower, the single morning each bloom opens.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: cured pod + seeds for flavour (ice cream, confectionery, baking); perfume and soap; folk
  medicine (aphrodisiac, carminative, stimulant). Green bean is odourless — the aroma is made by
  weeks of curing (kill, sweat, slow-dry).

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: calcium oxalate crystals in the sap → contact dermatitis in workers who pollinate/pick
  daily. Gloves. (Not a food hazard — a handler's one.)

## Inferences
- The one-morning flower + absent pollinator makes this the sharpest case of the cherimoya lesson:
  a food forest does not run itself; some of the best things want a hand on the plant's schedule.

## Anecdotes
- 1841, Réunion: Edmond Albius, a 12-year-old enslaved boy, devised the hand-pollination every grower
  still uses — the moment vanilla became a crop of the whole tropics. Second-costliest spice after
  saffron, and the labour is why.
