# Clausena lansium — raw

**Identity:** wampi, wampee. Rutaceae (citrus family). PSF role: sub-canopy, fruit. Copy entry #36.
A well-behaved citrus relative — no weedy streak.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1/2 · sub-canopy fruit tree** · lifespan long (a fast-growing
evergreen, then decades; first crop 5–8 yr from seed). Slower to first fruit than most here, then
reliable — a mid-canopy secondary.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **2,000–3,000 mm → 79–118 in**; tolerated **1,000–4,000 mm →
  39–157 in** (copy's 40–157 tracks).
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **22–30 °C → 72–86 °F**; tolerated **12–35 °C → 54–95 °F**; brief
  frost to ~−2 °C (28 °F), killed ~−7 °C (19 °F).
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,450 m → ~8,040 ft** (copy's 8,000 ft tracks).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich loams best; tolerates deep sand + oolitic limestone; well-drained; pH
  **5–6**, tolerated 4.5–6.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed germinates in days; greenwood + semi-ripe cuttings, air layering, veneer grafting all
  work. First flowering + fruit **5–8 yr from seed**. Up to ~45 kg fruit a season on a mature tree.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: grape-sized yellow fruit in loose clusters, juicy + aromatic, sweet to sour by tree — fresh,
  dried, preserved, or juiced. In southern China said to cut the richness of heavy meals.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none reported.

## Inferences
- Wide rainfall + high-altitude tolerance make it one of the more placeable fruit trees; the only real
  cost is the slow 5–8 yr start, which argues for planting it early behind faster returns.

## Anecdotes
- Southern Chinese + SE Asian, brought with Chinese migration — a quiet plantation-era dooryard fruit,
  unknown to most of the island. A good example of the buried diversity already here.
