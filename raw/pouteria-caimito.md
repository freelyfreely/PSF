# Pouteria caimito — raw

**Identity:** abiu. Sapotaceae. PSF role: sub-canopy to canopy fruit. Copy entry #57. Kin to
canistel and lucuma (same genus).

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 2 · sub-canopy/canopy fruit tree** · lifespan long (decades;
grafted 3–4 yr, seedling to 8 yr). A slower-arriving canopy fruit that wants accumulated life beneath it.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: **not specified (GAP)**. Wants year-round moist, warm, no dry stress.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: frost-intolerant; year-round moist and warm required.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: sea level–1,500 m → **0–4,900 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: prefers acid soil; grows well in wet soils (unusual for a good fruit tree).
- **Light** — Ferns: succeeds in full sun and in dappled shade.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed germinates 4–6 wk fresh; side-grafting; air-layering possible.
- Seedlings up to 8 yr to fruit; grafted 3–4 yr.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: fully ripe fruit to ~10 cm, translucent white jelly-like flesh — caramel-custard flavour,
  low acid, eaten fresh with a spoon.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: **eat fully ripe** — under-ripe fruit bleeds a milky latex that sticks to lips/mouth. Thin
  skin bruises; keeps only a few days.
