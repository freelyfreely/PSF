# Malpighia emarginata — raw

**Identity:** acerola, Barbados cherry, West Indian cherry. Malpighiaceae. PSF role: shrub, fruit
(vitamin C). Copy entry #27. **The worked example of the recrystallization pass** — Ferns thin,
resolved against Morton.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · dryland fruiting shrub** · lifespan decades (fruit 2–3 yr,
then successive flushes for years). Small (2–8 ft) and shear-tolerant but long-lived — a hedge-scale
secondary, not a placenta.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: thin/unstated. **Morton (*Fruits of Warm Climates*): adapted to medium- and
  low-rainfall regions; tolerates long drought; may not fruit until the rains come.** → repositions
  it toward *drier* ground and a wet-dry rhythm, not the wettest coast.
- **Temperature** — Morton: mature bush takes a brief **28 °F (−2.2 °C)**; young plants die below
  **30 °F (−1.1 °C)**. Tropical/subtropical.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: to 1,000 m in southern Mexico → **to ~3,300 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Morton: limestone, marl, and clay alike if well-drained; pH ≥ 5.5, better 6.5 for
  root development.
- **Light** — Ferns/Morton: full sun. Shrub 2–8 ft, takes shearing into a hedge.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed; half-ripe wood cuttings with entire leaves under bottom heat. First fruit 2–3 yr.
- **Cropping** — Ferns: "2–3 crops/yr." **Morton (Hawaiʻi-specific): a spring crop ~May, then small
  flushes off and on to ~December** — successive bloomings, not distinct annual crops. Morton's
  local reading wins for the copy.

## Uses / food — by source
- Cherry raw / juice / jam / jelly / wine.
- **Vitamin C (the disagreement worth keeping):** Ferns: 1–4 g per 100 g juice. Morton (per 100 g
  edible): **4,500 mg green → 3,300 medium-ripe → 2,000 fully ripe** — i.e. *falls as it ripens*.
  Still many × an orange at every stage. Grown industrially for the extract.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns/Morton: none known.

## Inferences
- The drought-adapted / hold-fruiting-until-rain habit + full sun says: a leeward or drier-Puna
  plant more than a wettest-windward one. The copy's earlier "not fussy, warm" understated this.

## Anecdotes
- West Indian / N. South American dooryard *vitamin bush* more than an orchard crop — on the list
  because it does not ship (soft, perishable), so only the grower meets it.
