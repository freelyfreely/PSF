# Curcuma longa — raw

**Identity:** ʻōlena, turmeric. Zingiberaceae. Common: turmeric. PSF role: ground / rhizome (spice).
Copy entry #3. Canoe plant; dye, medicine, and spice in one rhizome.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2** · lifespan: herbaceous perennial with a dormant season
(rhizome 9–12 mo). Fills the shaded floor of a young system; the dormancy is not death.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,000–2,000 mm → 39–79 in**; tolerated **800–3,000 mm → 31–118 in**.
  (Shipped band `40–80 in` = Ferns preferred, confirmed; copy's "takes 30 and takes 120" = the tolerated.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **20–28 °C → 68–82 °F**; tolerated **18–32 °C → 64–90 °F**.
  (Shipped "68 to 82°F" = Ferns optimal, confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 6,600 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: fertile, well-drained; pH pref 6–7, tol 5.5–7.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: **succeeds in full sun and in light shade.**

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: rhizome division during dormancy (leaves emerge ~30 days); seed sown fresh, germinates best
  at 20 °C. Crop harvested when leaves wither, **270–365 days**. (Shipped `food:9`.)

## Uses / food — by source
- Rhizome (boiled, dried, ground to powder; grated fresh). Young shoots and rhizome tips raw or cooked.
- The dye and the medicine predate the culinary use (dye for monks' robes; Hawaiian purification).

## Hazards — by source
- None of note. Stains skin and cloth for days (cosmetic, not a hazard).
