## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **seed usually breeds true**, viable 2–3 years dry; germinates ~21 days with bottom heat,
  ~40 ambient. Cuttings of mature wood root in ~28 days with heat. First fruit **3.5–5 years**, peak
  **years 5–10.** (Shipped `first food year 4–5`.)
- **Hand-pollination required** — Ferns: the flower is protogynous (female then male at different
  hours) and few insects visit; pollinate in the **6–8 hour window while stigmas are white and sticky**,
  every 4–5 days. A tree left alone sets a handful of fruit.

## Uses / food — by source
- White flesh, sweet, heavily perfumed, spooned raw; into ice cream, custard, cake. Runs to ~8 in.

## Hazards — by source
- **Seeds toxic** — Ferns: ~**0.15 g of the seed resin produces atropine-like poisoning**; juice in an
  eye can blind. Frame-independent — stated flat. Spit the seeds.
