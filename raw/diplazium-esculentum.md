# Diplazium esculentum — raw

**Identity:** hōʻiʻo (pohole on Maui), warabi, vegetable fern. Athyriaceae. PSF role: ground layer,
wet shade / frond (green). Copy entry #42. **Gathered, not planted** — the recognition crop. **Not**
bracken (*Pteridium*), which carries a carcinogen.

**Succession (axis 2):** *Not placed on the planting axis* — a gathered wild green, already present.
Ground layer, indefinite via spore and rhizome. Its "succession" is that it got there first; the
praxis is recognition, not planting ([PRAXIS.md](../PRAXIS.md) I).

## Climate & site — by source
- **Ferns entry is thin — no numeric climate bands.** Ferns gives only: altitude **below 900 m
  (~2,950 ft) in Sri Lanka**, mature height 0.5 m, "grows best with abundant organic matter." **No
  rainfall or temperature band exists.**
- **The shipped bands are lived / gathered-plant knowledge, not raster:** wet, shaded, mucky ground —
  streambanks, ditches, seeps, taro-patch edges; warm, frost-free; lowlands up shaded gulches to
  **~4,000 ft** (a lived Hawaiʻi figure, *higher* than Ferns' Sri Lanka 900 m). Shade to dappled sun.
  Labeled lived per [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md) tier 2 — the published record is genuinely absent here.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **spores** (germinate readily) or **division of root plantlets.** Copy: chiefly gathered; to
  establish a patch, transplant a rooted clump into wet shade — it naturalises and spreads by spore.
  Harvest young coiled fronds and tips year-round; picking encourages new growth.

## Uses / food — by source
- Young fronds (fiddleheads + tender stem) — raw in the local warabi salad (blanched, tomato, onion,
  dried shrimp) or stir-fried; crisp, mild, clean green snap. One of the few tropical ferns eaten
  freely across Asia and the Pacific.

## Hazards — by source
- **The hazard is misidentification, not the plant.** Bracken (*Pteridium*), tough and hairy, carries
  a carcinogen — a different fern. "Gather what you can name." Frame-independent; named.

## Inferences
- The purest form of the project's idea: food you do not plant, tend, or buy, already thick in the
  wettest, shadiest "useless" corner. *[The [PRAXIS.md](../PRAXIS.md) recognition case.]*
