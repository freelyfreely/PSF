# Averrhoa carambola — raw

**Identity:** star fruit, carambola. Oxalidaceae (wood-sorrel family). PSF role: sub-canopy fruit.
Copy entry #58.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: pref 1,200–2,000 mm, tol 600–3,000 mm → **47–79 in pref, 24–118 in tol**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: opt 24–30 °C, tol 5–39 °C, killed ≤ −2 °C → **75–86 °F opt, 41–102 °F tol,
  killed at 28 °F**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: to ~1,200 m → **to ~3,900 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, slightly acid; intolerant of waterlogging; pH 5.5–6.5, tol
  5–8.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: prefers a position with some shade.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed, greenwood cuttings, budding, grafting, layering. Grafted 1–2 yr; seedling 3–8 yr.
- Up to 3 crops/yr; flowering continues year-round.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: fruit raw (sliced into stars); flowers (raw/conserves); leaves (raw/cooked).

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: oxalic acid, esp. in the wing edges — mildly laxative; stings a sore mouth. Seed noted
  emmenagogue/abortifacient.
- **Medical literature (flat, NOT held lightly):** **caramboxin**, a neurotoxin. Healthy kidneys
  clear it; **renal-impaired patients cannot** → hiccups, confusion, seizures, death. Anyone with
  kidney disease must avoid it. The frame does not change this fact — stated flat, named early.

## Inferences
- The renal caution is the counterpart to katuk: both are food hazards, but this one is
  frame-independent (a real toxin, real physiology), so it is stated flat, not held at dose-size.

## Anecdotes
- SE Asian handsome dooryard tree, spread everywhere warm. The other wet-lowland gap-filler beside
  the abiu.
