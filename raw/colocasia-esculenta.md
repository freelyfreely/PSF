# Colocasia esculenta — raw

**Identity:** kalo, taro. Araceae. Common: taro, dasheen, eddoe. PSF role: ground / corm (starch).
Copy entry #2. Canoe plant; the genealogical elder (Hāloa).

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2** · lifespan: evergreen perennial (corm 7–10 mo, cut leaves
throughout). A longer-cycle placenta staple; starts under the closing canopy rather than in open sun.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,800–2,700 mm** → **71–106 in**; tolerated **1,000–4,100 mm** →
  **39–161 in**. (Shipped band `rain:[70,105]`, `rainTol:[39,161]` — Ferns-derived, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **21–28 °C → 70–82 °F**; tolerated **10–35 °C → 50–95 °F**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,700 m → ~8,860 ft**. (Shipped `elev:[0,8900]`.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: fairly heavy, fertile, moisture-retentive, organic-rich; pH pref 5.5–6.5,
  tol 4.3–8.2. Some cultivars tolerate high salinity.
- **Light** — Ferns: best in sun, tolerates light shade.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: division of suckers, corm pieces, or apical portions with attached leaf stalks (the *huli*);
  suckers set 50–75 mm deep. Corm 6–18 mo, avg **7–10 mo** (shipped `food:8`). Leaves harvestable
  through the season.

## Uses / food — by source
- Corm (poi, steamed), young leaves (lūʻau, must be cooked). All parts need cooking.

## Hazards — by source
- **Raphides (calcium-oxalate) throughout; cooking destroys them.** Frame-dependent (preparation) —
  held at true size: the plant is a staple, not a hazard, once cooked. Raw = acrid, throat-irritant.

## Inferences
- Two cultivation modes not separable in a single band: **wetland (loʻi)** kalo runs to the wet end,
  **dryland (ʻāpuʻa)** kalo to the drier/upland end. The Ferns band spans both; the real fit is
  cultivar-dependent. *[Inference — flag for the lived/CTAHR pass.]*

## Anecdotes
- The genealogical plant: Hāloa born, buried, returned as the first kalo, elder sibling to the first
  person — the loʻi as family relationship (copy "carried"). *[Lived / ethnobotanical — texture.]*
