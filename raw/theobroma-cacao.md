# Theobroma cacao — raw

**Identity:** cacao, chocolate tree. Malvaceae. PSF role: understory tree / pod (seed + pulp). Copy
entry #44. The guild-closer — the understory fruit the nitrogen-fixing nurses were planted for.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 2 · understory (guild-closer)** · lifespan: productive for
decades in the right shaded pocket. Planted **into** the nurse's ~50% shade (Inga, gliricidia,
banana), never open ground — the reason those nurses earn their "a snack" / "no crop" lines.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,500–2,000 mm → 59–79 in**; tolerated **480–4,300 mm → 19–169 in**.
  (Shipped `50–120 in` sits inside the tolerated band; the 50-in floor is slightly below Ferns'
  preferred 59 but well within tolerance — a wet-shade tree, comfortable on the Puna coast.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: mean annual **18–28.5 °C → 64–83 °F** (max 30–33.5 °C, min 13–18 °C);
  **absolute minimum 10 °C → 50 °F, below which severe damage.** (Copy "genuinely tender, unhappy
  below ~60 °F", confirmed — it is the tenderest tree on the list.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: lowland, **usually below 300 m → ~980 ft**, occasionally to **900 m → ~2,950 ft**.
  (Shipped "best under 1,000 ft, to about 2,000", confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: fertile, moisture-retentive, well-drained; **prefers acid soil**, pH 5–6.5,
  tol 4–8.
- **Light** — Ferns: **understorey tree — dappled shade, even quite dense shade** (copy: ~50%).

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **seed loses viability in 5–7 days** off the pulp (cannot be stored); germinates 7–10 days;
  grafted plants sooner and truer. Slow to establish (rarely >1.5 m after 2 yr). **Cauliflorous** —
  pods on trunk and old wood from **year 3–4** (shipped, confirmed), then continuously.

## Uses / food — by source
- Two foods per pod: sweet-tart white pulp (sucked fresh or juiced) and the seeds = cacao (fermented
  ~1 week under leaves, dried, roasted, ground). Hawaiʻi is the only US state growing it as a crop;
  lower Puna and Hilo sit within range.

## Hazards — by source
- None of note (as prepared).
