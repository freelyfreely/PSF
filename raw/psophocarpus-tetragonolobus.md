# Psophocarpus tetragonolobus — raw

**Identity:** winged bean, goa bean, four-angled bean. Fabaceae. PSF role: legume vine,
nitrogen-fixer, whole-plant food. Copy entry #55.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 1 · nitrogen-fixing legume vine** · lifespan: grown annual (pods
60–80 d, tubers 120–240 d, seed 6–9 mo). A maximum-density placenta that fixes nitrogen while it feeds
— the legume organ of the placenta layer, kin in role to pigeon pea but shorter and climbing.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: pref 1,000–2,500 mm, tol 500–4,100 mm → **39–98 in pref, 20–161 in tol**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal 18–30 °C, tol 14–40 °C, survives ~3 °C → **64–86 °F opt,
  57–104 °F tol, ~37 °F survival**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: to 2,000 m in tropics → **to ~6,600 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained loamy, slightly acid pref; tolerates heavy; pH 5.5–7, tol
  4.3–8.5. **Dislikes both wet soils and drought** — steady moisture with drainage.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny. Most kinds flower near a 12 h daylength (this latitude gives it).
- **N-fixation** — Ferns: exceptional — "greater weight of nodules per plant than any other legume."

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: pre-soak seed 12 h warm; sow direct; emergence 5–7 d; optimal germ ~25 °C.
- Young pods 60–80 d; mature tubers 120–240 d; seed 6–9 mo.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: pods, immature + mature seeds, roots/tubers, leaves, shoots, flowers/buds — nearly the
  whole plant. Mature seed ~protein/oil like soybean; tuber ~20 % protein (several × cassava/yam).

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none known.

## Inferences
- Dislikes wet feet → on the wet coast it wants a mound/slope, like pigeon pea and katuk. The
  "supermarket on a stalk" completeness (grain + green + root + N) is its whole argument.

## Anecdotes
- New Guinea / SE Asian dooryard climber; 1970s agronomy fame as a tropical soybean answer that
  faded — a generous forgotten everything-crop.
