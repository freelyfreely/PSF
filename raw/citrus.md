# Citrus (orange · grapefruit · lemon) — raw

**Identity:** sweet orange (*Citrus sinensis*), grapefruit (*Citrus × paradisi*), lemon (*Citrus
limon*). Rutaceae. PSF role: sub-canopy, fruit. Copy entry #48–50 (treated as one crop, three
flavours). **This entry fills the numbering that MAP flagged as a gap — the citrus *is* #48–50; the
corpus is not missing three entries.**

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 2 · sub-canopy fruit tree** · lifespan long (single trees to
~100 yr, orchards economic ~30; first fruit 3–5 yr). A durable mid-canopy fruit, grafted onto
rootstock — a structural secondary, not a placenta.

## Climate & site — by source (Ferns: *Citrus sinensis* as the proxy)
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: **no annual band given**; note "dry periods > 3 months → irrigation
  necessary." **The copy's "40–60 in" is NOT a Ferns figure** — flag as general/other, source or
  relabel. What Ferns states is a *seasonality* preference, not a quantity.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **25–30 °C → 77–86 °F**; coldest-month min ≥15 °C (59 °F); **growth
  ceases below 13 °C (55 °F) and above 38 °C (100 °F)** — the copy's "stalls below 55 °F and above
  100 °F" is exactly this.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: **not specified** (copy's "to 3,000 ft" is general/other — flag).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: deep, well-drained, moisture-retentive loam; **intolerant of waterlogging**;
  pH preferred **5–6**, tolerated 4.3–8.3.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun.
- **Seasonality** — Ferns: prefers "a prominent change in the seasons"; does better subtropical than
  tropical — the basis for "a little off the wettest, warmest coast."

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: grafted onto rootstock is standard (disease resistance + known fruit); some kinds
  polyembryonic + come true from seed; half-ripe cuttings + layering work. 6–13 m tree, fruit
  4–12 cm. First fruit **3–5 yr**; single trees to ~100 yr.
- Per-type (copy/general, not Ferns bands): lemon most everbearing + cold/neglect-tolerant; orange
  wants warmth + a cool spell to colour + sweeten; grapefruit wants the most heat + longest hang.

## Uses / food — by source
- Fresh + juiced; peel candied/flavouring; flowers as tea. Vitamin C + the acid brightness a
  starch-and-greens diet lacks.

## Hazards / pest picture — by source (CTAHR / USDA APHIS, per [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md))
- **Citrus greening (HLB):** the **Asian citrus psyllid vector has been established statewide since
  2003; the disease itself has never been found in Hawaiʻi**, and surveillance works to keep it out.
  Sources: CTAHR PD-112; USDA APHIS; state surveillance. **Held lightly for a diverse dooryard tree**
  (a pest's monoculture-orchard weight ≠ its dooryard weight — [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md)); the
  sensible care is **clean grafted stock**, not alarm. Everyday nuisances: scale + mites.

## Inferences
- Citrus is the counter-example the tool teaches with: a Kona orange export before coffee, undone by
  market + disease risk — the argument for the dooryard tree + mixed planting over the orchard of one
  thing. The pest note is the worked case of the "pest is a monoculture word" judgment.

## Anecdotes
- SE Asian, spread through the Mediterranean then the Americas; came to Hawaiʻi early (Kona oranges a
  real early export).
