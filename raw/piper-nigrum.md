# Piper nigrum — raw

**Identity:** black pepper. Piperaceae. PSF role: understory vine, spice. Copy entry #51.
Kin to *Piper methysticum* (ʻawa, #43) — same genus, same shade.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: pref 2,500–4,000 mm, tol 2,000–5,500 mm → **98–157 in pref, 79–217 in tol**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: best 22–35 °C, tol 10–40 °C → **72–95 °F best, 50–104 °F tol**. No frost.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: best < 500 m, to 2,000 m → **< 1,650 ft, to 6,600 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: neutral, rich in organic matter; pref pH 6–7, tol 5–7.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: "grows best in sheltered positions in semi-shade." Needs a living support to climb.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: woody cuttings ~45 cm root within 2 months (standard). Seed germinates 2–3 wk from fresh
  shade-dried berries. Sucker/stolon division delays flowering ~2 yr.
- First crop 3–4 yr; full production ~7 yr; economic life 12–20 yr.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: peppercorns (dried fruit) — black (green, dried), white (ripe, soaked, skinned), green
  (brined). Medicinal for digestion and fever; essential oil for flavour and perfume.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none known.

## Inferences
- 98–157 in pref lands squarely on the windward wet coast; the wet-shade-under-canopy niche is the
  same as ʻawa's, so it slots into the same understory the food forest already builds.

## Anecdotes
- Historically *money* — rents, city fortunes, and the Age of Exploration turned on cutting out the
  Malabar middlemen. Colour for the "Carried" beat, not a cultivation fact.
