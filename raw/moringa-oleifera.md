# Moringa oleifera — raw

**Identity:** moringa, drumstick tree, malunggay. Moringaceae. PSF role: sub-canopy / leaf (protein).
Copy entry #19. A stick pushed in the ground → edible leaves in months, eight feet by year's end.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · fast pioneer** · lifespan: fast-growing, coppice-managed
(leaf in months, 3–4 m/yr). Cut low to keep the leaf in reach — a nurse-speed tree grown as a green.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **700–2,200 mm → 28–87 in**; tolerated **400–2,600 mm → 16–102 in**.
  (Shipped `28–87 in` pref / `16–102 in` tol, confirmed. Wants the dry side.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **20–35 °C → 68–95 °F**; tolerated **7–48 °C → 45–118 °F**.
  Cold-hardy to light frosts; **killed to the ground by a freeze** (resprouts from light frost).
  (Shipped "68 to 95°F, takes 45 to 118", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: sea level to **~1,000 m → ~3,280 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 3,300 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, wide range; best on sandy/clay/clay loam; tolerant of **shallow,
  low-fertility** ground; pH 5.5–7, tol 5–8.5. **Not salt-tolerant.**
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **large cuttings** (100–150 cm long, up to 4 cm thick) root in a few months — the gliricidia
  trick. Seed no pretreatment, sprouts 1–2 weeks; ~80% fresh, 50% at 12 months, **dead by 2 years.**
  Growth **3–4 m/yr** for young plants. First fruits from cuttings **6–12 months**; seed-grown flower
  at 2 years. Leaf harvest starts within months. (Shipped `food:3` for leaf.)

## Uses / food — by source
- Leaves the crop (~7–10% protein dried, mustard note, raw or cooked). Flowers raw/cooked/tea. Young
  pods (6–18 in) like asparagus, or pickled. Immature seed like peas; mature roasted like peanuts.
  Root a pungent horseradish (too much is a problem). Seed presses an olive-like oil.

## Hazards — by source
- Root eaten sparingly (pungent). Otherwise none of note.

## Inferences
- Cut it to keep the leaves in reach — otherwise it goes to 25 ft and the food is in the sky (the
