- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **21–25 °C → 70–77 °F**; **does not set well above 32 °C → 90 °F**;
  **not frost-tolerant.** (Shipped "70 to 77°F; above 90 it stops setting; frost ends it", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 6,600 ft` — puts it almost
  anywhere on this island. Confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: humus-rich, fertile, well-drained loam; light sandy, slightly acid; pH pref
  5.5–5.6, tol 4.3–8.3.
- **Light** — Ferns: **very warm, sunny position.**

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed in a seedbed/container, up in **3–4 weeks at 20 °C**; plant out at **8–10 cm**. Self-
  fertile, insects handle it. **First harvest ~3 months from sowing** (shipped `food:3`).
- **Perennial** — Ferns: a perennial, often grown as an annual; left standing it makes a waist-high
  bush that fruits for years. Picking is the pruning.

## Uses / food — by source
- Very hot; raw, cooked, dried, or ground. Seed grinds as pepper too. Leaves a careful potherb.

## Hazards — by source
- Capsaicin heat only (culinary). None of note.
