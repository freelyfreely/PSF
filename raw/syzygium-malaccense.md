# Syzygium malaccense — raw

**Identity:** ʻōhiʻa ʻai, mountain apple, Malay apple. Myrtaceae. PSF role: canopy / fruit. Copy entry
#13. Shares a name with ʻōhiʻa lehua and is a wholly different tree — worth saying once.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 2** · lifespan: multi-decade canopy (first flower 4–5 yr, full
yield ~10). A slow, long fruit tree that reads the year and flowers on wet-after-dry.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,200–1,600 mm → 47–63 in**; tolerated **1,000–2,200 mm → 39–87 in**.
  (Shipped `47–63 in` pref / `39–87 in` tol, confirmed. The wettest windward ground is past what it
  prefers — the copy's windward caveat.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **22–28 °C → 72–82 °F**; tolerated **16–33 °C → 61–91 °F**;
  **frost-intolerant**. (Shipped "72 to 82°F", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,200 m → ~3,940 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 3,900 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich, well-drained; **dislikes very acid soils** (rules out much young lava);
  pH pref 5.5–6.5, tol 5–8.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed (fresh, does not keep), air layering (the usual way), cuttings, grafting/budding. Young
  trees transplant easily — unusual for a tree this size. **First flower 4–5 years; max yield ~10
  years; 60–80 kg/tree.** (Shipped `first food year 4–5`.)
- **Flowering trigger** — Ferns: **initiated by wet weather following a dry spell** — up to 3× a year,
  some years almost continually. "The tree reads the year and decides."

## Uses / food — by source
- Raw mostly — bell-shaped, more water and crunch than sugar. Stewed with cloves; baked in pies.
  Flowers preserved/salad; sour stamens raw; young red shoots raw with rice.

## Hazards — by source
- None of note.

## Inferences
- The acid-soil dislike + the drier-than-windward preference place it off the wettest, sourest ground —
  a fit caution, not a poor. *[Inference from the bands.]*

## Anecdotes
- Wood into house beams and bowls; bark and root a reddish-brown kapa dye. *[Ethnobotanical — texture.]*
