# Morinda citrifolia — raw

**Identity:** noni, Indian mulberry. Rubiaceae. PSF role: shrub to small tree / fruit (medicine,
famine food). Copy entry #38. Canoe plant; the colonization-system food plant — see
[INDICATORS.md](../INDICATORS.md) I.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · colonization-system pioneer** · lifespan: effectively
permanent (self-seeds, persists). Defined more by the *site axis* than by class — it is the type
food plant of raw substrate (bare ʻaʻā, pāhoehoe cracks). "Where noni grows, the succession has
already begun."

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,500–3,000 mm → 59–118 in**; tolerated **700–4,200 mm → 28–165 in**.
  **Discrepancy — the shipped `10–150 in` floor is far below Ferns.** 10 in (~250 mm) is a **lived
  extreme**, not Ferns: the steward's Kalapana walk found noni fruiting on bare rain-channeled
  pāhoehoe ([INDICATORS.md](../INDICATORS.md) I). Label the 10-in floor lived/observed, not raster.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **24–30 °C → 75–86 °F**; tolerated **12–36 °C → 54–97 °F**. Frost-free.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,500 m → ~4,920 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 1,300 ft` is a **conservative
  lived ceiling** — "occasionally higher"; Ferns allows much more.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained sandy; pH 5–6.5, tol 4.3–7. **Withstands salt-laden winds;
  established plants very drought-tolerant.** Copy adds standing-wet tolerance (lived).
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun to partial shade (fruits best in the open).

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed in nursery beds, germinates **3–9 weeks**, viable ≥6 mo; cuttings root easily.
  **Discrepancy on time-to-fruit:** Ferns says *"flowering and fruiting start in the third year from
  seed"*; shipped/copy says **first fruit within ~1 year.** The fast figure is cutting-grown / lived
  Puna — flag for confirmation (Elevitch / lived). Then year-round, all sizes at once.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ripe fruit eaten, juiced, fermented — sharp, bitter, notoriously foul-smelling. Young leaves wrap
  fish or cook as a mild green; green fruit curried. Famine food + daily tonic; root/bark a dye.

## Hazards — by source
- **High potassium → a real caution with kidney trouble** (copy). *Not from Ferns* — from the
  nutrition/medical record; recorded as a health caution, not a horticultural one. Frame-independent
  for a person with renal disease; named.

## Inferences
