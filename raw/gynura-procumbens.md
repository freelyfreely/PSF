# Gynura procumbens — raw

**Identity:** longevity spinach, sabuṅgai, sambung nyawa. Asteraceae. PSF role: ground layer, leaf
(green). Copy entry #23. One of the two proprietary greens whose bands are **lived**, not published.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · low sprawling perennial green** · lifespan years (cut-and-
come-again off one planting; first leaf 6–8 wk; layers as it runs). Nearly permanent once it takes —
a long-cropping ground-layer green, not a placenta annual.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall / Temperature / Altitude** — Ferns: **cultivation details "Not known" — NO published
  climate bands.** Only a habitat note (by streams / on shrubs at ~200 m in China). The climate line
  in copy is therefore **lived Puna ground-truth, labelled as lived** (per [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md)
  authority tier 2): thrives in the wet, sun or ~50 % shade, wants nights above ~50 °F, no dry season
  needed, frost-free. **Not a raster reading.**
- **Soil** — lived: well-drained ground with some organic matter; tolerant of poor soil.
- **Light** — lived: sun to ~50 % shade (bigger, softer leaf in shade).

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Cuttings of half-ripe wood root in soil or water — very easy. First leaves 6–8 wk, then for years.
  A low semi-succulent sprawler that layers where stems meet ground.

## Uses / food — by source
- Young leaves + tips raw or lightly cooked — thick, mild, faintly bitter, a little succulent. Also
  folk medicine across SE Asia (fever, "the blood"), the source of the "join the soul to the body"
  names. No food hazards reported.

## Hazards — by source
- None reported as a food. (Folk-medicinal claims are not carried as horticulture or as health advice.)
