# Gliricidia sepium — raw

**Identity:** gliricidia, madre de cacao, mouse-killer. Fabaceae. PSF role: canopy / support — **no
food**. Copy entry #11. Here to work: nitrogen, mulch, shade, living fence.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · support (no crop)** · lifespan: long, held short by coppice.
A pioneer for degraded and grass-choked ground; all its value handed to neighbours with a knife.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,200–2,300 mm → 47–91 in**; tolerated **600–3,500 mm → 24–138 in**.
  (Shipped `47–90 in` pref / `24–138 in` tol, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: best **15–30 °C → 59–86 °F**; tolerated **12–44 °C → 54–111 °F**;
  **killed at −2 °C → 28 °F**. (Shipped "59 to 86°F, takes 54 to 111, killed at 28°F", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,600 m → ~5,250 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 5,200 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: wide range, **alkaline and acidic**, low-fertility, calcareous; prefers free
  drainage but tolerates some waterlogging; establishes on **slopes to 40%**. pH pref 5.5–6.2, tol 4.5–8.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny. Its own canopy is light and feathery — what's under it still gets light.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **seed 90–100% germination in 7 days** (viability ~12 months open storage); nursery-plantable
  in ~3 months. **Even very large cuttings root** in the open ground — a whole branch becomes a fence.
- Coppice/pollard: tolerates lopping, browsing, fire; resprouts vigorously. ~3 m/yr once established.

## Uses / food — by source
- **The flowers, cooked. That is the whole edible list.** Wood durable, termite-resistant, burns hot.

## Hazards — by source
- **Toxic to humans, dogs, and horses** (leaves, seeds, powdered bark) — Ferns. Cows and goats eat it
  with impunity. Fermented with grain it is a rodenticide (the name means mouse-killer). **Frame-
  independent — stated flat**, per [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md): not dinner, keep it from the dog.
