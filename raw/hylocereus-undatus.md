# Hylocereus undatus — raw

**Identity:** dragon fruit, pitaya, white-fleshed pitahaya. Cactaceae. **Now placed in
*Selenicereus undatus*** in current taxonomy (Ferns lists it there) — note for cross-read. PSF role:
climbing (cactus) vine, fruit. Copy entry #32.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · climbing epiphytic cactus** · lifespan long (a perennial
cactus cropping for many years; first fruit 1–2 yr from a cutting). A durable vine-stratum member on
a living/dead post — not a placenta despite the fast first crop.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: **300–3,500 mm → 12–138 in** (an enormous range) — **but fruit sets poorly in
  the wettest areas** despite the plant thriving there. This is the load-bearing caveat: it *grows*
  wet, it *fruits* drier.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: best **18–28 °C → 64–82 °F**; tolerated **12–34 °C → 54–93 °F**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft** (copy's "coast to 6,600 ft" tracks).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, **pH 6 or lower**; preferred 5.3–6.7, tolerated 5–7.5. Rots in
  heavy wet soil.
- **Light** — full sun; throws aerial roots and needs a support (living post, wall, old tree).

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: stem cuttings root readily (the standard way); seed viable but slow + off-type. First fruit
  1–2 yr. **Night-blooming, bat/moth-pollinated flowers open a single night** — in wet areas it grows
  lush and sets little, so growers hand-pollinate after dark.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: red/pink skin over white or magenta flesh flecked with tiny black seeds, mild + sweet, eaten
  raw + chilled; unopened flower buds cooked as a vegetable. Mostly water + texture — refreshment more
  than calories.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none. Note: **considered invasive in some regions** — stems root wherever they touch down.

## Inferences
- The wet/dry split makes this a **leeward + drier-Puna-pocket** crop by fruiting behaviour even
  though it survives the wet coast — the same "grows here, won't fruit here" pattern the tool must be
  able to voice, and the copy already does.

## Anecdotes
- Central American climbing cactus turned supermarket fruit; on this island a leeward + backyard crop,
  a recent arrival grown chasing the fruit's high price — a different motive from most of the list.
