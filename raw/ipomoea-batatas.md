# Ipomoea batatas — raw

**Identity:** ʻuala, sweet potato. Convolvulaceae. PSF role: ground / tuber (starch). Copy entry #1.
Canoe plant; the leeward staple.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 1** · lifespan: perennial, cropped as an annual (tuber ~2–3 mo).
The max-density soil crop — placenta, the organ that grows the forest, not filler before the trees.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **750–2,000 mm → 30–79 in**; tolerated **350–5,000 mm → 14–197 in**.
  (Shipped `rain:[30,80]`, `rainTol:[14,197]` — confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **18–28 °C → 64–82 °F**; tolerated **10–38 °C → 50–100 °F**.
  Dormant roots survive to ~1 °C; young growth killed at 5 °C.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: to **2,800 m → ~9,180 ft** in the tropics. (Shipped `elev:[0,9000]`.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained sandy loam, ample potash; pH pref 5–7, tol 4–8.7.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun necessary. Short-day for flowering; day length barely affects tubers.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: terminal stem cuttings (slips), or seed (scarify/pre-soak). Tropical maturation as fast as
  **~2 months**; subtropical ≥3 mo. (Shipped `food:3`.)

## Uses / food — by source
- Tuber (staple starch); young leaves and shoots edible cooked (a good green).

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none of note. (Leaf is a common cooked green across the Pacific.)

## Inferences
- The drought-and-poor-soil tolerance (350 mm floor, sandy loam) is why ʻuala farmed the leeward
  slopes kalo could not — the two staples partition the island by water. *[Inference from the bands +
  ethnobotany.]*

## Anecdotes
- Pre-Columbian presence in Polynesia centuries before any European carriage from the Americas — a
  return voyage east and back. The plant handed over the fence. *[Lived / ethnobotanical.]*
