# Dioscorea alata — raw

**Identity:** ʻuhi, water yam, greater yam. Dioscoreaceae. PSF role: climbing vine / tuber (starch).
Copy entry #41. Canoe plant; the third starch beside kalo and ʻuala. **Not** the wild toxic hoi
(*D. bulbifera*) — plant the named cultivar.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · climbing tuber** · lifespan: seasonal vine, tuber regrows from
a left sett. A staple placenta grown up a stake, the kō, or a tree — the vertical starch crop.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,000–1,500 mm → 39–59 in**, evenly distributed **after a 4–5 month
  dry season**; no tolerated band given. (Shipped `40+ in` = the preferred floor. Note the dry-season
  nuance: Ferns wants a dry spell; copy renders it as "steady moisture… and a drier spell to finish.")
- **Temperature** — Ferns: **26–34 °C → 79–93 °F**. Not frost-tolerant.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,000 m → ~3,280 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 2,600 ft` is conservative.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: deep, well-drained sandy loam; pH pref 5.5–6.5, tol 4.8–8.5. Needs support.
- **Light** — Ferns: not specified (shipped "sun to part shade").

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **setts** — small tubers cut into 2–4 sections, larger into 6–8, each with 2–3 dormant buds;
  aerial bulbils also. Harvest **7–10 months** (shipped/copy 8–10 ✓). Copy: vine climbs 15+ ft then
  **yellows and dies back to signal harvest**; leave a sett and it regrows; stores months cool/dry.

## Uses / food — by source
- Tuber boiled/baked/steamed/pounded — dense, starchy, mild; white to deep-purple flesh (purple prized).
  Always cooked. The keeping starch, cured and stored against lean months.

## Hazards — by source
- None for *D. alata* itself. **The caution is identity:** the volunteer wild hoi (*D. bulbifera*) is
  bitter and toxic until long-leached — plant the named ʻuhi, not the volunteer. Frame-independent
  (different species); named.

## Inferences
- The dry-spell-to-finish (Ferns' 4–5 mo dry season) is a real tension with the always-wet windward
  coast — the tuber finishes cleaner where the year gives a drier close. *[Inference from Ferns' band.]*
