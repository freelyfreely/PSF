# Piper methysticum — raw

**Identity:** ʻawa, kava. Piperaceae. PSF role: understory shrub / root (drink, medicine, ceremony) —
**not food**. Copy entry #43. Canoe plant, sterile and sacred; Puna is kava country.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · understory** · lifespan **15–30 yr** (Ferns). Shade-grown
beneath the food canopy; the patch renews itself indefinitely from cuttings — the understory as a
layer that yields.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **2,200–4,000 mm → 87–157 in**; tolerated **1,800–5,000 mm → 71–197 in**.
  **Note:** shipped `60+ in` sits *below* Ferns' tolerated floor (71 in) — ʻawa wants it genuinely wet,
  wetter than "60+" implies; Puna's wet ground suits it. Read the floor as ~71 in, not 60.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **22–35 °C → 72–95 °F**; tolerated **14–40 °C → 57–104 °F**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **800 m → ~2,620 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 2,000 ft`, confirmed-ish.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, stony; pH pref 5.5–6.5, tol 5–7.
- **Light** — Ferns: **shade when young**, older plants more sun-tolerant. Copy: brittle jointed stems
  want wind shelter.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns lists "seed" and cuttings — **but *P. methysticum* is sterile and seedless** (ethnobotanical
  record, [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md) tier 1–2, corrects Ferns' generic *Piper* boilerplate):
  propagated by **stem cutting only**, by hand, for ~3,000 years — every plant a carried clone.
  Harvest root **3–4 years** (Ferns: ~1 kg/plant at 10 mo, profitable after 3–4 yr). Replant a cutting.

## Uses / food — by source
- **Not food.** Root (fresh or dried) pounded, kneaded in water, strained into an earthy, peppery,
  tongue-numbing drink for calm, sleep, sociability, ceremony. Named Hawaiian cultivars vary in strength.

## Hazards — by source
- **Heavy, prolonged use dries and scales the skin** (kava dermopathy) — a condition that **reverses
  when use eases**; the traditional water preparation has a long safe-use record. Frame-dependent
  (dose/duration), held at true size. *Ferns notes only "used in excess… can cause stupor"; the skin
  caution is from the ethnographic/medical record, not Ferns.*
