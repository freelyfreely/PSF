# Sauropus androgynus — raw

**Identity:** katuk, sweetleaf, star gooseberry. Phyllanthaceae. PSF role: shrub, leaf/protein.
Copy entry #54. (Also placed in *Breynia androgyna* in recent taxonomy — note for cross-read.)

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · understory leaf shrub** · lifespan years (plant once, cut for
years; first leaf 55–70 d). The chaya / aibika pattern in a third family — a long-cropping perennial
green, not a placenta annual.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: pref 900–1,200 mm, tol 600–4,000 mm → **35–47 in pref, 24–157 in tol**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal 26–35 °C, tol 5–38 °C → **79–95 °F opt, 41–100 °F tol**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: to 4,000 m → **to ~13,000 ft** (nominal; realistically coast to upland here).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: tolerates heavy soils, well-drained preferred, does not need rich ground;
  pH 6–7 opt, tol 5.5–7.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun and semi-shade.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed germinates readily; cuttings of somewhat woody shoots 20–30 cm establish easily.
- First harvest 55–70 days.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: leaves raw or cooked; young shoots (~7 cm, asparagus-like); ripe fruit → sweets; green
  food dye. Protein 6–10 %.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none known.
- **Medical literature (flat-but-narrow):** a 1990s Taiwan slimming fad of drinking large amounts of
  *raw* katuk juice daily caused **bronchiolitis obliterans** (lasting lung injury). Not a hazard of
  the cooked vegetable in normal amounts — a hazard of *dose + raw preparation*. Held at true size:
  cook it, eat it; do not juice it by the pitcher.

## Inferences
- Prefers drier (35–47 in) but tolerates the wet coast (to 157 in) with drainage — same pigeon-pea
  logic: on the wettest ground, mound or slope it.

## Anecdotes
- Dooryard green from Borneo/Malaya through South India; the chaya/aibika archetype in a third
  botanical family — the leaf you walk past and pick, for years, off one planting.
