# Elettaria cardamomum — raw

**Identity:** cardamom (green/true). Zingiberaceae. PSF role: understory spice. Copy entry #52.
Kin to *Curcuma longa* (ʻōlena, #3) and *Zingiber officinale* (ʻawapuhi, #4) — same family, wants
what they want turned up: more shade, more cool, more height.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: pref 2,500–3,800 mm, tol 1,500–5,800 mm → **98–150 in pref, 59–228 in tol**.
  Drought reckoned < 125 mm/month.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal annual mean ~22 °C → **~72 °F**; diurnal 10–35 °C → **50–95 °F**;
  min ~17 °C annual mean → **~63 °F**. Above 24 °C, vegetative propagation only.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: typical cultivation 600–1,500 m → **~2,000–4,900 ft** (the coffee band).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich, moist, well-drained forest loam; drainage essential.
- **Light** — Ferns: partial shade, ~50 % optimal; cannot tolerate prolonged direct sun.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed germinates 5–7 wk (irregular, up to 1 yr); division also viable and truer.
- Bearing begins ~3 yr after planting (4–5 yr from seed); economic production 10–15 yr.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: seeds/fruits (spice); young shoots; leaves (wrapping); rhizomes; essential oil.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none known.

## Inferences
- The altitude + shade bands *place* this plant: it is a coffee-belt understory crop, not a coastal
  one. The record should not offer it as a wet-coast plant.

## Anecdotes
- "Queen of spices"; by weight among the costliest. Wild in the Western Ghats understory — the
  instruction for growing it is "recreate the forest floor it came from."
