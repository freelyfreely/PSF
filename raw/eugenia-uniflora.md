# Eugenia uniflora — raw

**Identity:** Surinam cherry, pitanga, Brazilian cherry (ambiguous — cf. grumichama). Myrtaceae. PSF
role: shrub, fruit. Copy entry #33. Kin to grumichama (#37, *E. brasiliensis*) — same genus, one
weedy and one well-behaved.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · slow fruiting shrub** · lifespan long (slow-growing, decades;
first fruit 2 yr at best, 5–6 usually). Suckers + bird-sown — a self-installing secondary that must be
held as a hedge (see arrested-state note).

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,200–2,000 mm → 47–79 in**; tolerated **700–2,700 mm → 28–106 in**
  (copy's 28–108 tracks).
- **Temperature** — Ferns: growth **12–32 °C → 54–90 °F**; optimal **21–27 °C → 70–81 °F**; withstands
  several degrees of frost, −5 °C superficial injury on older plants.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,800 m → ~5,900 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: fertile, moist, high-organic; **intolerant of alkaline ground** (one source);
  acid side. Deep-rooted, drought-tolerant once established.
- **Light** — Ferns: sun to part shade; full sun for best shape.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed viable ~1 mo, 80 % up in 20–50 d; layering + grafting work. First fruit 2 yr at best,
  usually 5–6. Fruit crops run 6–8 wk, several times a year. Slow, 6–40 ft.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: small ribbed red-to-black cherry, one seed, flavour swinging **sweet-spicy to peppery/
  resinous by individual plant** — taste before planting a hedge. Fresh or jam; one fruit ~a day's
  vitamin C. Resinous seeds not eaten.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none as a food (resinous seed bitter, not eaten).
- **Arrested-state / weed note** (cf. [INDICATORS.md](../INDICATORS.md) IV): **suckers into dense
  thickets, spreads by birds + roots — a listed weed in some regions.** Manage as a clipped hedge;
  pull the volunteers.

## Inferences
- The guava double-edge again: easy, generous, does not stay put. Choose the plant for its fruit
  (flavour is plant-specific), keep it sheared, and treat new plantings as a decision with
  consequences downstream.

## Anecdotes
- Brazilian; spread worldwide as an ornamental hedge, which is mostly what it is here — a fruiting
  hedge people prune without knowing the fruit is good.
