# Basella alba — raw

**Identity:** Malabar spinach, vine spinach, Ceylon spinach. Basellaceae. PSF role: vine, leaf
(green). Copy entry #22. The hot-season green — leafy where lettuce bolts.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 1/2 · climbing leaf vine** · lifespan short-lived perennial here
(treated cut-and-come-again; crop ~70 d). A fast, maximum-density soft-leaf producer grown up a
string — the leaf placenta of the vine stratum.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **2,000–2,500 mm → 79–98 in**; tolerated **700–4,200 mm → 28–165 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **23–27 °C → 73–81 °F**; tolerated **10–35 °C → 50–95 °F**; minimum
  ~15 °C daytime (~59 °F) for vigorous growth. Not frost-hardy.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,500 m → ~4,920 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: sandy loam, well-drained, moisture-retentive, organic-rich; pH preferred
  **5.5–7**, tolerated 4.3–7.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: warm sunny sheltered; **larger, more succulent leaves in light shade**. Long days
  keep it from flowering, so it stays leafy at this latitude.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed pre-soaked a day (up 10–21 d in warmth), or 8-in cuttings that root any time of year.
  Needs support — stems run to ~30 ft. Crop at ~70 days; 40 t/ha in 75 days on record.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: leaves + tips raw or cooked — mild, mucilaginous (thickens a stew like okra); vitamins A + C,
  iron, calcium. Handles the 90 °F heat that ends most leafy greens.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none reported as a food.

## Inferences
- Tolerated 28–165 in spans the whole windward coast; its real niche is the hot months, so it reads as
  a season-filler (the green for the heat) more than a year-round staple.

## Anecdotes
- The hot-season green across South + Southeast Asia, grown up the side of a house; the red-stemmed
  form half ornamental.
