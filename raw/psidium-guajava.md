# Psidium guajava — raw

**Identity:** kuawa, guava. Myrtaceae. PSF role: sub-canopy / fruit. Copy entry #16.
"The argument this whole project is having, standing in a field" — already here, already fruiting,
and a noxious weed across the tropics.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · self-seeding pioneer** · lifespan ~40 yr (heavy 15–25, fruit
yr 1–2). Fast, bird-sown, suckers from the root — usually already standing before anyone plants it.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,000–3,000 mm → 39–118 in**; tolerated **400–5,000 mm → 16–197 in**.
  (Shipped `40–120 in` pref / `16–197 in` tol, confirmed. Wants a dry stretch to ripen.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **23–28 °C → 73–82 °F**; flowering needs 3.5–6 months above
  **16 °C → 61 °F**; dormant to **−5 °C → 23 °F**, young growth damaged at **−1 °C → 30 °F**.
  (Shipped "73 to 82°F, dormant takes 23°F, young burns at 30", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: fruits to **1,500 m → ~4,920 ft**; survives to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft**.
  (Shipped "fruits to 4,900 ft, survives to 6,600", confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: fertile, moisture-retentive but tolerant; pH 5.5–7.5, tol 4–8.5.
  **Moderately salt-tolerant; more drought-resistant than most tropical fruit.**
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed germinates **15–20 days**, comes true; cuttings/air-layer/graft (clonal fruits in year 1).
  Mostly it is **already standing** — the work is choosing which stems to keep. Seedlings flower within
  **2 years**, full bearing **5–8**. (Shipped `first food year 1–2`.)
- **Yield / lifespan** — Ferns: 30–40 kg at 5 yr, 50–70 kg max at 7; heavy bearing years 15–25; ~40-yr life.
- **Weediness** — Ferns: "often escapes cultivation and is considered a noxious weed." Suckers from
  roots and self-sows freely.

## Uses / food — by source
- Raw, skin and all — vitamin C several times an orange. High pectin (sets jam unaided). Seed oil;
  leaves in cooking and tea.

## Hazards — by source
- None to the eater. The *hazard is ecological* — its spread, and its worse cousin strawberry guava
  eating the native forest.

## Inferences
- The honest position (copy): harvest and cut back what has already arrived and already fruits; moving
  it into new ground is a decision with consequences that outlast the gardener. *[A stance, not a band —
