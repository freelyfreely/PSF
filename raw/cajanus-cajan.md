# Cajanus cajan — raw

**Identity:** pigeon pea, gandule, toor dal. Fabaceae. PSF role: shrub / legume (protein) + nitrogen.
Copy entry #6. The nurse that dies on purpose. Corroborated by Götsch (see below).

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · pioneer legume** · lifespan **1–5 yr** (Ferns), dies and
hands over. Shorter-lived than a typical secondary 1 but plays its role — fast, soft, coppices,
retires. Götsch places *Cajanus cajan* among accumulation-system species that bridge toward abundance.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: **~500–1,000 mm → 20–40 in** per year. (Shipped `20–40 in`, confirmed.)
  *Windward caveat is real: this coast gives more than it wants — needs a mound or slope so water leaves.*
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **20–30 °C → 68–86 °F**; good yields up to **35 °C → 95 °F**.
  (Shipped "68 to 86°F, yields to 95", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: lowland tropics up to **1,500 m → ~4,920 ft**; some forms to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft**.
  (Shipped `coast to 4,900 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: wide range if well-drained; **dislikes very acid soils**, best pH **5.5–6.5**.
  (The real limit on young volcanic ground — sour ground, not rain.)
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny position.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: sow in situ in the wet season, seed **2–3 cm deep**; germinates 19–43 °C, fastest 20–30 °C;
  emergence complete **2–3 weeks** after sowing. Harvest: dwarf varieties from **5 months**, taller
  **8–12 months**. (Shipped `food:5`.)
- **Nitrogen fixation** — Ferns: symbiotic root-nodule bacteria fix atmospheric nitrogen.
- **Lifespan** — Ferns: short-lived, **dies after 1–5 years**. The death is the hand-off (copy).

## Uses / food — by source
- Green peas (shelled, ~5 min) or dry (soaked, simmered — dal, rice-and-peas). Up to ~5 t/ha pods.
  Leaves edible (strong, go fibrous fast). Dry stalks burn at ~half the heat of coal.

## Hazards — by source
- None of note.

## Inferences
- Slow at the start, so it does not out-compete a seedling tree planted the same day — the nurse
  timing. Roots break a soil pan; water starts going down again. *[Inference from habit.]*

## Anecdotes
- No wild pigeon pea exists anywhere — it has only ever lived in the company of people, and was never
  once the plant anyone came for. *[Ethnobotanical — texture.]*
