# Musa — raw

**Identity:** maiʻa, banana / plantain. Musaceae. PSF role: sub-canopy / fruit (starch). Copy entry #9.
Copy ships *Musa* spp. (many cultivars); bands here are read from **Musa acuminata** as the Ferns
proxy — the A-genome parent of most edible bananas. *[The species stand-in is itself a flag: cultivar
bands differ, and a lived Puna reading across apple banana / Williams would sharpen this.]*

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · perennial clumping mat** · lifespan: each stem ~15–18 mo
(fruits once, dies), the mat indefinite. High-biomass pioneer — windbreak then chop-and-drop mulch.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,200–3,600 mm → 47–142 in**; tolerated **650–5,000 mm → 26–197 in**.
  (Shipped `47–140 in` pref / `26–197 in` tol, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal growth **23–33 °C → 73–91 °F**; tolerated **12–42 °C → 54–108 °F**;
  optimal fruiting ~27 °C; **ripening stalls below 18 °C → 64 °F**; **killed at 1 °C → 34 °F**.
  (Shipped "73 to 91°F, killed at 34°F, won't ripen below 64°F", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: natural to **1,200 m**, cultivated to **2,400 m → ~7,870 ft**.
  **Discrepancy:** shipped band says `coast to 4,000 ft` — a narrower, Hawaiʻi-practical ceiling than
  Ferns' 2,400 m. Recorded as a **lived/practical narrowing**, not a Ferns figure; keep the label.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained fertile; pH pref 6–7.5 (also given 5.5–7.5), tol 4–8.4.
- **Light / wind** — Ferns: sunny, **sheltered** — wind shreds the leaves and it stands useless.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: sucker division (seed only for wild types, ~20 °C, slow). Ferns gives no time-to-fruit.
  **CTAHR (cross-read, 2026-07-17): "days to harvest 15–18 months from planting"** in Hawaiʻi;
  flowering Feb–Apr, fruit matures 3–5 months after flower; early varieties picked May–June, late
  mid-July–Sept. So the shipped `first food year 1–1.5` is **optimistic at its low end — CTAHR's
  Hawaiʻi figure is 15–18 months (~year 1.25–1.5).** Correction: read the low end as ~15 months, not
  12. Stem fruits once then dies; the mat is perennial (keep 2–3 followers, cut the rest).

## Uses / food — by source
- Ripe raw or green cooked (the cooking types fed people). Flower raw/roasted; young shoots in sauces;
  stem core like bamboo shoot; leaf as wrap/plate/imu lid.

## Hazards — by source
- None of note.

## Inferences
- The spent stem is ~90% water and rots to mulch in weeks — the windbreak-then-mulch role on the
  windward side of young ʻulu/cacao. *[Inference from habit; a guild-pass placement.]*

## Anecdotes
- Most Hawaiian maiʻa were kapu to women for most of Hawaiian history; what grows here now is mostly
  post-contact cultivars (apple banana, Williams). *[Ethnobotanical — texture.]*
