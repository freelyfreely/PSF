# Hibiscus sabdariffa — raw

**Identity:** roselle, Jamaica sorrel, flor de Jamaica, bissap. Malvaceae. PSF role: shrub, calyx
(drink/tart) + leaf. Copy entry #56. Kin to *Abelmoschus manihot* (aibika, #20) — another generous
hibiscus grown for a soft edible part.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: opt 1,500–2,000 mm, tol 640–4,290 mm → **59–79 in opt, 25–169 in tol**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: 12.5–27.5 °C annual → **55–82 °F**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: sea level–~600 m → **0–2,000 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained humus-rich; friable sandy loam opt; adapts widely; pH 4.5–8.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun, **not shade-tolerant**. Short-day: no flowering > 13 h daylight;
  flowers near 11 h → **the calyx crop comes as the year shortens (fall/winter)**.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed (soak/abrade to speed), or half-ripe cuttings. First harvest 3–5 mo. Grown as an annual.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: calyces (red/green forms) → drink, jam, sauce, pastry; young leaves + stems (lemony
  potherb); seeds (roasted). Root edible but fibrous.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: "oil may contain toxic substances" — the *seed oil*, not the calyx/leaf food. Minor.

## Inferences
- The short-day trigger is load-bearing for a Hawaiʻi grower: plan the calyx harvest for the
  shortening days, not midsummer. Full-sun requirement keeps it out of the deep-guild understory.

## Anecdotes
- African hibiscus, tropical-belt dooryard drink/relish. Hawaiʻi ʻUlu Cooperative co-plants it with
  māmaki + breadfruit — exactly the food-guild company it keeps.
