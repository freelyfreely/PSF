# Alternanthera sissoo — raw

**Identity:** sissoo spinach, Brazilian spinach, *samba lettuce*. Amaranthaceae. A **sterile
cultivar of *Alternanthera sessilis*** — and that parent is a serious Hawaiʻi weed; sissoo sets no
seed and stays put, which is the whole point. PSF role: ground cover + leaf (green). Copy entry #24.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · edible groundcover** · lifespan perennial mat (effectively
permanent, doesn't run off by seed; first leaf ~2 mo). Doubles as the **groundcover stratum** — the
living-mulch layer under everything else, which is a stratum job as much as a food one.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall / Temperature / Altitude** — **no published climate band** (a sterile ornamental-edible
  cultivar; Ferns/PFAF carry the parent species, not usable bands for this clone). Climate is **lived
  Puna ground-truth, labelled lived** (per [SOURCES.md](../SOURCES.md) tier 2): grows effortlessly
  year-round, sun or heavy shade, wet ground included; frost-tender; lowland to mid-elevation. **Not a
  raster.**
- **Soil** — lived: well-drained, high in organic matter.
- **Light** — lived: thrives in **50 %+ shade** (unusual for a leaf crop) and also takes full sun.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Cuttings only (it is sterile) — roots at the nodes as it runs; very easy. Shoots at 15–25 cm,
  picked regularly; harder cutting raises yield. A spreading perennial ground cover ≤ ~1.5 ft,
  matting and weed-excluding.

## Uses / food — by source
- Leaves + tender shoots raw or cooked — **crunchy, not mucilaginous** (unlike Malabar / aibika), a
  clean mild spinach. Raw in salad, or steamed/boiled. Can take insect leaf damage in spots.

## Hazards — by source
- None as a food. The one caution is **identity**: do not confuse with the weedy fertile parent
  *A. sessilis* — the sterility is what makes the cultivar safe to grow here.

## Inferences
- Being sterile is the succession point: it fills the groundcover stratum and holds soil without the
  runaway-by-seed risk its parent carries — a designed pioneer that cannot become the weed.

## Anecdotes
- Came through the tropical-permaculture world as the near-perfect edible ground cover: feeds you,
  shades the soil, keeps weeds down — three jobs from one low green mat.
