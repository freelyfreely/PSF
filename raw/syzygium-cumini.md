# Syzygium cumini — raw

**Identity:** jamun, java plum, black plum. Myrtaceae. PSF role: canopy, fruit. Copy entry #34. Kin
to ʻōhiʻa ʻai (#13, *S. malaccense*) and rose apple (#35, *S. jambos*).

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · fast canopy fruit tree** · lifespan long (fast to establish
— 13 ft in 2 yr — then decades; first fruit 4–6 yr). A vigorous, bird-sown pioneer-canopy tree; its
speed + self-sowing put it near the secondary-1 pioneers despite its final size.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,500–6,000 mm → 59–236 in**; tolerated **800–9,900 mm → 31–390 in**
  (copy's 60–235 tracks the preferred band). Tolerates prolonged flooding **and** 6–7 mo dry.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **20–32 °C → 68–90 °F**; tolerated **12–48 °C → 54–118 °F**; killed
  ~−2 °C (28 °F).
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft**.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: versatile, grows on shallow rocky ground with rain; pH **5.5–7**, tolerated
  4.5–8.
- **Light** — Ferns: sun; moderately shade-tolerant when young.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed 1 in deep in the rains, up 2–4 wk; air-layering + grafting for known fruit; plant
  seedlings out at 1–3 yr. First fruit 4–6 yr. Fast — 13 ft in 2 yr, 30–50 ft grown.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: olive-shaped purple fruit, juicy + astringent (better after a salt-water soak) → jam, juice,
  tarts, vinegar; quality variable tree to tree. **The seed, dried + powdered, measurably lowers blood
  sugar — a standard traditional diabetes treatment across India.** The stain marks hands, clothes,
  pavement.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none as a food.
- **Weed note** (cf. [INDICATORS.md](../INDICATORS.md) IV): **self-sows freely — a listed pasture pest
  in Florida + elsewhere.** Manage the seedlings.

## Inferences
- Flood- *and* drought-tolerant + huge rainfall range = one of the most site-forgiving fruit trees on
  the list; its constraint is weediness + astringency, not climate. Its pioneer speed is a placement
  asset (fast shade/biomass) and a liability (spread) at once.

## Anecdotes
- South Asian, venerated, temple-planted; came with Indian + plantation-era migration then spread on
  its own — the tree that purples old roads every summer, fruit nobody harvests underfoot.
