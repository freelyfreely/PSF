# Pipturus albidus — raw

**Identity:** māmaki. Urticaceae (nettle family, stingless). **Endemic to the Hawaiian archipelago —
the only native plant in the PSF set.** Role: native understory shrub, leaf (tea). Copy entry #59.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · native understory shrub** · lifespan years–decades (evergreen
6–15 ft; leaf crop from ~1–2 yr, ongoing). The endemic understory member of the guild — the class is a
label on a native holding its own forest-floor niche.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — **no published horticultural band.** Read by forest type, not a raster: a plant of
  coastal mesic, mixed mesic, and wet forest. (Native range, not a reading.)
- **Elevation** — Native Plants Hawaiʻi / HEAR: 60–1,830 m → **200–6,000 ft** (unusually wide native
  range).
- **Temperature** — warm, frost-free; not suited to hot, dry coastal ground.
- **Light** — CTAHR/NPH: part shade; red-veined forms handle open sun better than green-leaved. An
  understory shrub for taller trees.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- ʻUlu Cooperative: seed is the usual way — soak ripe white fruit in non-chlorinated water ~48 h,
  shake seeds free; ~3 mo under mist; grow on in part sun to ~12 in; plant out.
- Leaves for tea within ~1–2 yr; evergreen shrub 6–15 ft, cropping for years.

## Uses / food — by source
- Leaves → māmaki tea (mild, earthy, caffeine-free); central to lāʻau lapaʻau (traditional Hawaiian
  medicine). Small white mulberry-like fruit edible, sweet-mild.

## Hazards — by source
- Fruit is a gentle laxative in quantity. No sting (island nettle disarmed — no browsing mammals to
  defend against).

## Ecology (native-record, load-bearing here)
- **Sole larval host of the pulelehua, the Kamehameha butterfly** (*Vanessa tameamea*) — one of only
  two butterflies native to Hawaiʻi. To plant māmaki is to feed a brew, a healing tradition, and a
  native butterfly at once.
- ʻUlu Coop guild: sited ~2–3 ft off breadfruit, with roselle + lemongrass alongside.

## Inferences
- Belongs at the seam where the food guild is built to hold the *native web* inside it, not just the
  carried crops — the one indigenous member, and the argument that succession can be built to
  include what was here before people.
