# Solanum pimpinellifolium — raw

**Identity:** currant tomato, wild/pimpinellifolium tomato. Solanaceae. **The wild ancestor all
cultivated tomatoes descend from.** PSF role: ground/sprawling, fruit. Copy entry #47.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 1/2 · self-seeding sprawler** · lifespan annual/biennial (Ferns),
but **effectively permanent by reseeding** here — fruit 2–3 mo, then it volunteers year after year.
A placenta that reinstalls itself, which is most of its argument.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall / Temperature / Altitude** — Ferns: **NO cultivation climate bands** ("found in tropical
  and subtropical S. America; not frost-hardy; grown as a spring-sown annual in warm-temperate
  zones"). This is **the one Part IV annual where Ferns is genuinely thin** — so the copy's climate
  line ("warm · coast to ~5,000 ft · sun") is **lived Puna ground-truth**, labelled lived, plus general
  Solanum knowledge. Not a raster.
- **Soil** — lived/general: ordinary well-drained soil, full sun.
- **Lifespan** — Ferns Properties: annual/biennial.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Seed, started or direct; **self-seeds readily and volunteers thereafter.** First fruit 2–3 mo, then
  continuously in warm weather.

## Uses / food — by source
- Currant-sized, intensely sweet red fruit eaten by the handful raw or cooked to sauce. Yield is in
  numbers, not size.

## Hazards — by source
- None as a food. (Foliage is nightshade — not eaten; general to the family.)

## Inferences
- Notably resistant to the late blight + humidity that flatten full-sized tomatoes in the wet tropics
  — the wild ancestor kept the toughness breeding traded for size. This resistance is a **grower/lived
  observation**, well attested in the literature but not sourced here to a specific paper — flag if it
  is ever stated as a hard fact.

## Anecdotes
- Coastal Peru + Ecuador wild tomato; a small lesson in why the wild/naturalised sometimes beats the
  improved on a humid island.
