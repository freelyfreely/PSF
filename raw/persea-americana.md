# Persea americana — raw

**Identity:** avocado. Lauraceae. PSF role: canopy, fruit (fat). Copy entry #25. Three races cover
the whole island, sea level to cool uplands — match the race to the ground.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 2 · canopy fruit tree** · lifespan long (many decades; grafted
fruit in year 2, seedling 6–8 yr). Wants accumulated life + free drainage beneath it; a structural
canopy member, not a placenta.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **500–2,000 mm → 20–79 in**; tolerated **300–2,500 mm → 12–98 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: overall **10–45 °C**, preferred **14–40 °C → 57–104 °F**. By race: Mexican
  opt 14–25 °C (57–77 °F), Guatemalan 15–28 °C (59–82 °F), West Indian 24–32 °C (75–90 °F).
- **Cold tolerance** — Ferns: dormant to ~**−4 °C (25 °F)**; young growth hit at −1 °C. Mexican race to
  **−6 °C (21 °F)**, Guatemalan to −4 °C — the source of the "Mexican for the uplands" placement.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,800 m → ~9,180 ft** (race-dependent). Copy's "coast to 9,000 ft" tracks.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich, neutral-to-alkaline, all soil types, **well-drained; intolerant of
  waterlogging**; pH preferred **5–5.8**, tolerated 4.5–7. **Root rot from wet feet is the main way it
  dies here** — drainage is not negotiable.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: grafted trees fruit in year 2, stay 8–10 m; seedlings go to 20 m, 6–8 yr, may not come true.
  **Self-sterile / dichogamous** — plant ≥2 trees, ideally an A-type + a B-type (flowers open female
  then male at different hours). Seed germinates ~1 mo.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: fat — 20–30 % oil in the Mexican race down to 3–10 % West Indian (the rich buttery ones are
  the cool-climate types). Eaten raw. **Ripens only off the tree** — mature fruit holds on the branch
  for weeks (the tree is the storage), which is the harvest signal: pick hard, ripen on the counter.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none for people. (Leaves/pit toxic to some animals — general note, not a food hazard.)

## Inferences
- The three-race structure is really a *climate-fit* device: the tool should, eventually, resolve
  avocado by elevation band into the right race rather than treating it as one plant.

## Anecdotes
- Central American, old (Nahuatl via Spanish). Half orchard, half feral here — the roadside seedlings,
  each different, known good ones by the tree, like the mangoes.
