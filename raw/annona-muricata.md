# Annona muricata — raw

**Identity:** soursop, guanábana. Annonaceae. PSF role: sub-canopy, fruit. Copy entry #26. The
lowland Annona that does *not* need the cold its cousin cherimoya (#17) demands.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · small sub-canopy fruit tree** · lifespan ~2–3 decades
(fruit from year 2; a fast small tree ~23 ft). A quick-returning secondary — sooner than most fruit
trees, shorter-lived than the big canopy species.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,000–2,500 mm → 39–98 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **25–30 °C → 77–86 °F**. Frost-intolerant.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **~1,000 m → ~3,280 ft** (copy's "coast to 3,300 ft" tracks).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: moist, well-drained sandy loam / deep rich loam; pH **5.5–6.5**; tolerates
  poor + light alkaline soils; established trees take some drought.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed breeds true; semi-ripe cuttings also work. First fruit year 2; a 5-yr tree carries
  10–50 fruits. Can flower year-round in fits. **Flowers protandrous → set poorly alone; hand-
  pollination lifts yield + fruit shape.**

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: big fruit (>2 lb), white fibrous pulp, sweet-sour — mostly juiced/blended. Immature green
  fruit cooked as a vegetable.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: **seeds toxic** (do not blend them into juice); stems carry an irritant sap.
- **Literature flag (not yet sourced here):** *Annona* spp. contain **annonacin**, an alkaloid linked
  in Caribbean (Guadeloupe) studies to **atypical parkinsonism** among heavy long-term consumers of
  the fruit/leaf/tea. This is a **frame-independent** class of hazard if it holds — but it is **not
  sourced in this record**; treat as a cross-read target (nephrology/neurology literature, as star
  fruit's caramboxin was), not a stated fact. Do **not** put it in reader copy until sourced.

## Inferences
- Soursop is the warm-lowland answer where cherimoya refuses; but the hand-pollination need makes it
  another "the forest wants your hand" case (cf. cherimoya, vanilla).

## Anecdotes
- Lowland Annona of the whole tropics, grown for juice more than the raw fruit; a large folk-medicine
  reputation (now being chased by researchers) is most of why it is spreading on this island now —
  which is exactly why the annonacin flag deserves a real cross-read.
