# Passiflora edulis — raw

**Identity:** lilikoʻi, passion fruit. Passifloraceae. PSF role: vine / fruit. Copy entry #8.
Named for Lilikoʻi Gulch on Maui, where it came over a fence.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · vine** · lifespan 3–6 yr (fruit yr 1–2, then spent).
Fast and short — a placenta of the vertical layer, cut to the ground and regrown or replaced.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **900–2,000 mm → 35–79 in**; tolerated **600–2,500 mm → 24–98 in**.
  (Shipped `35–80 in` pref / `24–98 in` tol, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **20–30 °C → 68–86 °F**; tolerated **18–34 °C → 64–93 °F**.
  Cold: mature dormant plants to **~−2 °C → 28 °F**; **young growth takes no frost**. (Shipped
  "68 to 86°F, mature takes 28°F", confirmed.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: subtropics **sea level–900 m → 0–2,950 ft**; tropics 900–2,000 m or higher.
  (Shipped `coast to 3,000 ft` = the subtropical ceiling, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: humus-rich, well-drained; pH pref 6–8, tol 5.5–8.5. **Moderate fertility
  only** — rich ground gives vine and no fruit.
- **Light** — Ferns: **dappled shade** (the forest edge, not an open fence). Sheltered from cold wind.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: cuttings from mature wood fruit the **same year**; seedlings **12–24 months** (seed can take
  ~12 months just to germinate). Layering nearly always works. Needs a trellis/fence/host.
  (Shipped `first food year 1–2`.)
- **Lifespan** — Ferns: commercial life **3–6 years**, then spent (cut to the ground, it comes back).

## Uses / food — by source
- Pulp and seed spooned raw; juice, syrup, baking. High vitamin C. Seeds press for oil. Leaves cooked
  rarely ("nobody does it twice").

## Hazards — by source
- None of note (culinary).

## Inferences
- The wet-end drainage caveat: at the wettest windward ground it wants sharp drainage. *[Inference
  from the "well-drained" + moderate-fertility habit.]*
