# Sechium edule — raw

**Identity:** chayote, **pīpīnola**, choko, mirliton. Cucurbitaceae. PSF role: vine, fruit (starch)
+ shoots + root. Copy entry #31. The definition of set-and-forget — one sprouting fruit at a fence.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · perennial climber** · lifespan perennial where frost-free
(renew ~every 3 yr for disease); immature fruit 3–4 mo, mature 4–6 mo, starchy root only in year 2.
A longer-cycle placenta that keeps giving — the whole plant is food across its run.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **800–2,000 mm → 31–79 in**; tolerated **600–2,600 mm → 24–102 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **19–30 °C → 66–86 °F**; tolerated **12–40 °C → 54–104 °F**;
  below 13 °C damages small unripe fruit; frost kills.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,000 m → ~6,560 ft**; **better yields above 500 m** (~1,640 ft).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, fertile, humus-rich; pH preferred **5.5–7**, tolerated 4.3–8.
- **Light** — Ferns: mostly full sun; some cultivars crop better in light shade.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: plant the **whole sprouting fruit**, widest end down, buried two-thirds; stem cuttings root
  in shade. Must have strong support — annual stems run 9–30 m. One plant: 80–100 fruits + 40–50 lb of
  root a year.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: the whole plant — pale fruit (cooked like squash or raw slaw), young shoots + leaf tips (a
  green), the seed (cooked), and in year 2 a starchy tuber (boil/bake/fry). Mild — carries the pot.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none reported.

## Inferences
- "Better above 500 m" + tolerates 6,560 ft = one of the few staple-ish crops that actually improves
  upcountry — worth pairing with the cooler-elevation recommendations, not just the coast.

## Anecdotes
- Mesoamerican; the Hawaiian name pīpīnola marks how long it has been naturalised — it runs wild over
  old homesteads and gulch fences on the wet side, a plant so easy it turned into scenery.
