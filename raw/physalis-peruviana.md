# Physalis peruviana — raw

**Identity:** poha, cape gooseberry, goldenberry. Solanaceae. PSF role: shrub, fruit. Copy entry
#30. The poha of poha jam — a Hilo/Volcano upland flavour.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · short-lived fruiting shrub** · lifespan evergreen perennial
harvestable "at least 3 yr" (Ferns), often grown shorter; first berries ~3 mo. A fast placenta-tier
fruit — quick return, brief life, self-replacing where it naturalises.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,500–2,300 mm → 59–91 in**; tolerated **800–4,300 mm → 31–169 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **16–25 °C → 61–77 °F**; tolerated **10–32 °C → 50–90 °F**; takes
  light frost. The mild, upland band — not the hot coast.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: **optimal in the tropics above 800 m → above ~2,625 ft**; overall range sea
  level–4,500 m. **CORRECTION: the copy reads "above 800 ft" — that is a metres→feet error.** Ferns'
  optimum is **800 m ≈ 2,600 ft.** The copy's own logic ("the upland, not the hot coast") only works
  at 800 m; 800 ft is still hot lowland. **Fix the copy to ~2,600 ft (800 m).**
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: humus-rich loam preferred, tolerates poor, dislikes clay; pH 4.5–8.2. Excess
  richness cuts fruiting.
- **Light** — Ferns: full sun or light shade, sheltered.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed (helped by a day–night temperature swing), planted out at 3–4 in; division also works.
  First berries ~3 mo, then harvestable ≥3 yr. Picked in the husk, keeps for months.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: golden berry in a papery lantern, sweet-tart, raw or into pie + jam; dried like a raisin.
  Rich in vitamins A + C, unusually high protein for a fruit.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: **the papery calyx (husk) is toxic — do not eat the wrapper.** The ripe berry is the food.
  (Unripe fruit + foliage are nightshade — not eaten.)

## Inferences
- The altitude correction matters for placement: poha is an **upcountry** crop (Waimea, Volcano,
  Kula-analog), not a wet-coast one. Recommending it "above 800 ft" would put it in exactly the hot
  lowland it underperforms in.

## Anecdotes
- Andean, a tomatillo relative, naturalised early + thoroughly enough to take a Hawaiian name; poha
  jam is a Hilo + Volcano thing. Most people meet a volunteer, not a planted crop.
