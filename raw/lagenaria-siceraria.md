# Lagenaria siceraria — raw

**Identity:** bottle gourd, calabash, **ipu**. Cucurbitaceae. PSF role: ground/climbing vine, fruit
(vegetable young) + vessel (mature). Copy entry #46. A canoe plant carried for the vessel more than
the meal.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 1 · annual climbing vine** · lifespan annual (tender young fruit
~3 mo; mature vessel gourds hardened on the vine, cured for weeks–months). A fast maximum-density
placenta whose "goodbye" is literal — the fruit is left to die into a container.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,200–1,800 mm → 47–71 in**; tolerated **700–2,800 mm → 28–110 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **19–27 °C → 66–81 °F**; tolerated **12–38 °C → 54–100 °F**.
  Frost-tender.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: economic crops below 500 m; grown up to **1,600 m → ~5,250 ft** (copy's "5,000
  ft" tracks this).
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich, moisture-retentive, well-drained, circumneutral; pH preferred **5.5–7**,
  tolerated 4.5–7.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: warm, sunny, sheltered from wind; an arbour so the gourds hang straight.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed pre-soaked, sown warm; **night-flowering, moth-pollinated.** Tender young fruit ~70–90 d;
  mature 100–120 d (some cultivars 60–80). For vessels, harden on the vine then cure dry.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: young green fruit a mild watery vegetable (stir-fry, soup, curry). Mature = the hard-shelled
  ipu, not food.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: **never eat a bitter fruit** — an occasional bitter individual carries a real toxin
  (cucurbitacins). Frame-dependent (taste tells you) but genuine; named plainly.

## Inferences
- Same finding as pumpkin: **Ferns is not thin here** — it carries full bands. The copy's lived-only
  framing for the annuals should be corrected to "Ferns for pumpkin + ipu; lived for currant tomato."

## Anecdotes
- The ipu heke of hula, the water gourd, the pre-pottery household container — it crossed the ocean
  for the vessel; that it is also food is almost beside the point of why it came.
