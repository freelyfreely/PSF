# Mangifera indica — raw

**Identity:** manakō, mango. Anacardiaceae. PSF role: canopy / fruit. Copy entry #14.
The leeward tree — needs a dry season the windward coast never gives.

**Succession (axis 2):** **climax** · lifespan **100+ yr** (peak ~20, decline after ~40). Deep-taproot
canopy that arrives last and stands longest — the far end of the successional axis on this list.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **600–1,500 mm → 24–59 in**; tolerated **300–2,600 mm → 12–102 in**.
  (Shipped `24–60 in` pref / `12–102 in` tol, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **24–30 °C → 75–86 °F**; tolerated **8–48 °C → 46–118 °F**;
  dormant to **−1 °C**, young growth damaged at **0 °C**. (Shipped "75 to 86°F, survives 46 to 118".)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: optimal to **1,200 m → ~3,940 ft**; **commercial max 600 m → ~1,970 ft**.
  (Shipped "up to 3,900 ft; under 2,000 for a real crop", confirmed both figures.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich, deep, well-drained (not poor or shallow); pH 6–7, tol 4.3–8.5. Deep taproot.
- **Dry-season requirement** — Ferns: **a marked dry or cool season of ≥3 months to promote flowering.**
  This is what puts it on the leeward side — the single most load-bearing fact in the record.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny; shelter from drying wind.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: grafted onto polyembryonic rootstock → bearing in **3–5 years**; seed-raised **5–8 years**.
  **Polyembryonic** forms throw seedlings identical to the mother (why a good mango travels by seed
  here, hand to hand). Germinates 18 days peeled / 30 unpeeled. (Shipped `first food year 3–5`.)
- **Lifespan** — Ferns: peak to ~year 20, declines after 40, **may live 100+ years.** Flowering irregular.

## Uses / food — by source
- Ripe raw; green as vegetable/pickle (amchur dried/powdered). Seed = famine starch (boiled/roasted).
  Young leaves cooked; flowers edible.

## Hazards — by source
- **Sap/skin contact → contact dermatitis like poison ivy** in sensitive people (Ferns). Frame-
  dependent (individual sensitivity) — named, held at true size.

## Inferences
- The dry-season rule, not rainfall alone, is the reason it is a "poor" fit on the wet coast in the
  site's logic — a plant that needs a dry season is poor on ground that never gives one. *[Inference
  linking Ferns' flowering trigger to the fit logic.]*
