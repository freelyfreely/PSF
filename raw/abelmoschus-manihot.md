# Abelmoschus manihot — raw

**Identity:** aibika, Tongan spinach, aibika/bele, sunset muskmallow. Malvaceae. PSF role: shrub,
leaf (green). Copy entry #20. Kin to *Hibiscus sabdariffa* (roselle, #56) — another generous hibiscus
grown for a soft edible part.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · fast leaf shrub** · lifespan short-lived perennial (turns
annual at its cool/high edge); leaf from ~3 mo, then continuous. Maximum-density soft-leaf producer,
prune-responsive — a leaf placenta, not a structural tree.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,000 mm or more → 39+ in**, evenly distributed. No firm upper
  band stated (a wet-tolerant plant).
- **Temperature** — Ferns: prefers a **stable mean above 25 °C → above ~77 °F**. Warm, humid.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: optimal **below 500 m → below ~1,640 ft** (yields drop above); native habitat
  in Nepal 700–1,700 m (2,300–5,580 ft). The copy's "coast to 1,600 ft" tracks the optimal band.
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, fertile, organic-rich; **specific pH not stated**.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny position.
- **Daylength** — Ferns: does not flower well when daylength < 12 h — stays leafy where days are long.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed in situ or cuttings from young shoots; a 5 m shrub if nothing stops it. Young shoots
  harvested at ~15 cm; Ferns gives no firm time-to-first-leaf (copy's "within 3 months" is the lived/
  grower figure — flag as such).

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: young leaves + growing tips (raw or cooked; mucilaginous, thickens like okra); flower buds.
  Up to 40–60 t/ha of leaf on good ground kept well.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none reported.

## Inferences
- Best below 1,600 ft + wants ≥39 in evenly spread = a wet-low-coast leaf crop; it tails off and may
  turn annual toward the uplands. Keep it out of upcountry recommendations.

## Anecdotes
- The everyday Pacific dooryard green (PNG, Fiji, Tonga, Vanuatu); a cultigen with no certain wild
  ancestor — the leaf equivalent of breadfruit, carried and kept by people.
