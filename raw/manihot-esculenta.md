# Manihot esculenta — raw

**Identity:** cassava, manioc, tapioca, yuca. Euphorbiaceae. PSF role: shrub / tuber (starch).
Copy entry #5. The famine plant that waits in the ground.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2** · lifespan 2–3 yr (root ~6 mo, holds in ground ~1 yr).
Götsch names *cassava* explicitly among placenta crops — planted dense, builds soil on worn ground.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: **500–2,500 mm → 20–98 in** per annum. (Shipped `20–100 in`; upper rounds
  Ferns' 98, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: minimum **16 °C → 61 °F**; growth adversely affected above **29 °C → 84 °F**.
  (Shipped "above 61°F, best under 86" — the 86 °F/30 °C ceiling is a rounding of Ferns' 29 °C caution.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: below **1,700 m → ~5,580 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 5,600 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: sandy or sandy loam, well-drained; tolerates poor, farmed-out ground; pH 5.5–8.0.
- **Light** — Ferns: needs **some shelter from bright sunshine** or the leaves burn. (Shipped "sun, sheltered".)

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: cuttings — shoots **~30 cm long, ~15 mm thick** — root readily in water or straight in the
  ground. Seed reverts to bitter types (not used). Roots mature in **~6 months**. (Shipped `food:6`.)

## Uses / food — by source
- Tuber (sliced/fried, boiled, flour, tapioca). Leaves edible cooked (carry the protein the root lacks).
  Root is carbohydrate and little else — a monodiet of it is a deficiency.

## Hazards — by source
- **Hydrocyanic acid (cyanogenic glucosides).** Frame-dependent (preparation) but *not* held lightly —
  it is a real toxin. Ferns: leaves boiled **~15 min** to destroy the glucosides; **bitter varieties
  soaked ~24 h** before cooking. Sweet types hold it in the outer layer (~10 min simmer); bitter types
  hold it throughout. "A cassava without its method is a poison — the knowledge is the crop."

## Inferences
- Tolerates heavy, staggered harvest and holds in the ground for up to a year, enlarging — the
  "famine plant waits" behaviour. *[Inference from habit + ethnobotany.]*
