# Aleurites moluccanus — raw

**Identity:** kukui, candlenut. Euphorbiaceae. PSF role: canopy / nut (oil, relish, light). Copy
entry #39. Canoe plant; the state tree, the tree of light, and a generous pioneer nurse.

**Succession (axis 2):** **secondary 1 · pioneer canopy / nurse** · lifespan: long-lived,
self-seeding. Fast, soft, self-sowing — takes bare ground, throws shade and mulch, nurses the slower
food trees while they climb. A native-range analogue of the Inga nurse role.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: **650–4,300 mm → 26–169 in** (single band, no pref/tol split). (Shipped
  `40–170 in` — upper matches Ferns' 169; the 40-in floor is a conservative lived reading, Ferns
  allows down to 26.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: mean annual **18–28 °C → 64–82 °F**; tolerates short frosts to **−4 °C → 25 °F**.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: below **1,200 m → ~3,940 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 2,000 ft` is conservative —
  "naturalised through the lower Hawaiian valleys.")
- **Soil / light** — Ferns: wide range if well-drained, pH 5–8; **sunny**; tolerant of strong wind
  and some salt spray.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **seed requires scarification** — the traditional method burns dry grass over a single layer
  of seeds, then plunges the hot seed into cold water to crack the shell. **First flowers ~4 years
  old** (shipped nuts by year 4, confirmed). Growth moderately fast, ~1.5 m/yr. Self-seeding, long-lived.

## Uses / food — by source
- Nut, **roasted only** → ʻinamona (pounded with salt, into poke/rice/poi). Oil for cooking and light.
  Non-food: lamp nuts, tattoo-ink soot, bark/flower medicine, dye.

## Hazards — by source
- **Fresh/raw seed is a violent purgative** — Ferns: "contains a principle resembling croton oil…
  should not be eaten"; also cyanide, **largely destroyed by thorough drying or cooking.** Frame-
  dependent (preparation) but a real toxin — roast before eating, never raw. Named early.
