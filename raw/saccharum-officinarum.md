# Saccharum officinarum — raw

**Identity:** kō, sugarcane. Poaceae. PSF role: tall grass / cane (sweet) + windbreak, mulch. Copy
entry #40. Canoe plant with dozens of named Hawaiian cultivars; the plantation's gentler twin.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2 · high-biomass structural grass** · lifespan: perennial clump,
ratoons several harvests before replanting. Windbreak-then-mulch, like banana — a pioneer that is
structure between cuttings and biomass when chopped.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,500–2,000 mm → 59–79 in**; tolerated **1,000–5,000 mm → 39–197 in**.
  (Shipped `60+ in` = the preferred floor; the wet windward coast is ideal. Confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **24–37 °C → 75–99 °F**; tolerated **15–41 °C → 59–106 °F**.
  Cold: below 10 °C long-term lethal; 0 °C chlorotic; −3 °C kills young cane; −11.5 °C kills all.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: sea level to **1,600 m → ~5,250 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 3,000 ft` is
  conservative.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: pH 5–8, tol 4.5–9; moderately salt-tolerant, relatively acid-tolerant. Heavy
  feeder that repays as biomass.
- **Light** — Ferns: sunny.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: **cane setts of 2–3 joints**, laid with only 2–5 cm above soil; eyes shoot in ~2 weeks. Copy:
  mature cane **12–18 months**, then **ratoons from the stool** for several harvests. Yield: mature
  irrigated 100–150 t cane, young 60–90 t.

## Uses / food — by source
- Cane chewed (split a joint) or crushed for juice. Named Hawaiian kinds bred for the chew, ceremony,
  and medicine — not a mill. Between harvests: windbreak, screen, trellis; chopped tops = potassium-
  rich mulch.

## Hazards — by source
- None of note.

## Inferences
- "One grass held two ways" — the dooryard kō (five jobs, takes nothing from away) vs. the plantation
  monoculture that reshaped the islands' water. *[Stance / texture, not a band.]*
