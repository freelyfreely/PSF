# Cucurbita moschata — raw

**Identity:** tropical pumpkin, butternut/field pumpkin, calabaza. Cucurbitaceae. PSF role: ground
vine, fruit (starch). Copy entry #45. The squash species that takes tropical heat + humidity where
thin-skinned kinds fungus.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 1 · annual ground vine** · lifespan annual (immature fruit +
shoots earlier; mature keeping fruit ~3–5 mo). Maximum-density fast starch crop that says goodbye —
a textbook placenta, sprawling over the open ground of a young system.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **600–1,600 mm → 24–63 in**; tolerated **300–2,800 mm → 12–110 in**.
- **Temperature** — Ferns: preferred **20–30 °C → 68–86 °F**; tolerated **10–40 °C → 50–104 °F**;
  killed at 0 °C. Optimal in fairly low humidity.
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **2,400 m → ~7,870 ft**. (Copy's "coast to 5,000 ft" is a conservative
  narrowing, not an error.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: rich, well-drained, moisture-retentive; pH preferred **5.5–7.5**, tolerated
  4.5–8.3.
- **Light** — Ferns: very warm, sunny, sheltered; plenty of room to run.

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: seed in mounds, thinned. **Minimum warm growing season ~150 days** for mature keeping fruit —
  i.e. nearer **~5 months**, not the copy's "three to four." Immature fruit + shoots come earlier.
  **Flag: the copy's 3–4 mo is optimistic for the keeping pumpkin; 150 d is the Ferns figure.**

## Uses / food — by source
- Ferns: mature flesh baked/boiled/soup; young fruit like summer squash; shoot tips + flowers a green;
  seeds roasted. Keeps for months without a fridge — the food-security point.

## Hazards — by source
- Ferns: none reported. (Rare bitter individuals carry cucurbitacins, as in all cucurbits — do not eat
  a bitter one; general to the family, not specific here.)

## Inferences
- **Correction to the copy's framing:** the intro to Part IV says the annuals' numbers are lived
  "where the Ferns entries are thin." **Ferns is *not* thin for *C. moschata*** — it carries full
  rainfall, temperature, altitude, and pH bands. The lived-only framing overstated the gap; this
  record can and now does cite Ferns. (Only the currant tomato is genuinely band-less — see
  [solanum-pimpinellifolium](solanum-pimpinellifolium.md).)

## Anecdotes
- American, old; *moschata* is the one that took to the wet tropics. In Hawaiʻi the field pumpkin runs
  half-wild over old homesteads like the chayote — forgiving, keeping, asks almost nothing.
