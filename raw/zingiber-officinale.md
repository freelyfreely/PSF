# Zingiber officinale — raw

**Identity:** ʻawapuhi (the culinary ginger; distinct from wild ʻawapuhi kuahiwi, shampoo ginger).
Zingiberaceae. Common: ginger. PSF role: ground / rhizome (spice). Copy entry #4. Canoe plant.

**Succession (axis 2):** **placenta 2** · lifespan: herbaceous perennial (rhizome ~10 mo, stalks
wither at harvest). Ground-layer placenta under a closing canopy.

## Climate & site — by source
- **Rainfall** — Ferns: preferred **1,400–3,000 mm → 55–118 in**; tolerated **700–4,000 mm → 28–157 in**.
  (Shipped band `55–120 in` = Ferns preferred; copy's "takes 28 to 157" = the tolerated, confirmed.)
- **Temperature** — Ferns: optimal **19–29 °C → 66–84 °F**; tolerated **13–35 °C → 55–95 °F**.
  (Shipped "66 to 84°F" = Ferns optimal, confirmed. Cold drives dormancy.)
- **Altitude** — Ferns: up to **1,900 m → ~6,230 ft**. (Shipped `coast to 6,200 ft`, confirmed.)
- **Soil / pH** — Ferns: well-drained, humus-rich, neutral to alkaline; pH pref 6–7, tol 4.3–7.5.
- **Light** — Ferns: **full sun or partial shade.**

## Propagation & timing — by source
- Ferns: division as new growth begins. **Ten-month growing season** from planting out a root,
  harvested when the stalks begin to wither. (Shipped `food:10`.)

## Uses / food — by source
- Rhizome (grated fresh; dried and powdered, ~twice as sharp). Young rhizome raw, pickled, or candied.
  Leaves and shoots as a potherb/wrap; flower heads raw.

## Hazards — by source
- None of note.
