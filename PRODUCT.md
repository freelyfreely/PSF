# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People on Hawaiʻi Island with a relationship to their land — local-kine folks, transplants, landscapers, policy-makers. Very few are ecologists; most have never heard the word "succession." They arrive on a phone, often on slow rural connections, thinking about their actual yard. The tool must be trustworthy enough to stake a garden on and inspiring enough to shift how they see their ground.

## Product Purpose

A recommendation tool (site + printable flyers) that reads the real rain and elevation at any point on the island and says which food plants fit that ground, in the order you would plant them — Prep (what the site needs), Set (how it goes in), Forget (what happens after, mostly pruning and letting things fall). The real product is the inner succession: the shift from seeing land as inert substrate to experiencing it as a living community. The plant recommendations are the delivery mechanism. Hawaiʻi imports over 90% of its food; the obstacle is not data but the cultural container.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, plain-spoken, quietly authoritative — a well-worn notebook in a garden. Speaks in the third person about plants; never instructs the reader. Concrete words (grow, cut, wait, harvest); Hawaiian names alongside common names; the technical term arrives only after the plain word has carried it.

## Anti-references

- The "tasteful AI editorial" kit: cream/parchment body, centered serif masthead, terracotta accent as decoration.
- SaaS patterns: hero metrics, identical card grids, uppercase tracked CTAs, gradient anything.
- The landscaping-gaze: land as a dead static rectangle. The design must not present plants as products in a catalog.
- Grandiose vocabulary: tapestry, paradigm, synergy, "dive in."

## Design Principles

1. **The surface is the soil.** Dark, rich, volcanic ground and living canopy greens carry the palette; warmth comes from earth, not from a cream default.
2. **Succession is structure.** The page darkens as the canopy closes; type grows with the plants; the year-clock is the spine. Nothing arrives all at once.
3. **Honest numbers, soft interfaces.** Live data labeled as live, derived data labeled as derived, and "nothing will feed you here" is a legitimate answer.
4. **The reader knows their own ground.** Poor fits are dimmed, not deleted. No lectures, no managed experience.
5. **Survives a photocopier.** Flyers are print-first; the site works on an old phone in Puna.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast throughout, including on tinted entry backgrounds. Keyboard path for everything the map does (town buttons mirror map clicks). `prefers-reduced-motion` honored. Works without accounts, tracking, or heavy assets.
