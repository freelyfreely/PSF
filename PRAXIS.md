# Praxis — the doing

Where the knowledge meets the hands and the attention. [succession.md](succession.md) holds the
physics of *why*; this holds the practice of *how* — both the outer technique and the inner shift
that is the project's real product. A living document; add to it from the ground.

The distinction that earns this file its own place: the other documents describe *plants*. This one
describes *the person acting on them*. Prep Set Forget is, underneath the plant list, a method for
changing how someone stands in relation to land — and a method is praxis, not data.

---

## I. The inner succession — the real product

From [psf-project]: the plant recommendations are the delivery mechanism; the actual deliverable is
an *inner* succession — a person moving from *land as inert substrate* to *land as living community*.
Praxis is where that move is made concrete, because it is made by doing, not by reading.

- **The pattern is the teacher.** Prep → Set → Forget is not just a planting sequence; it is a
  retraining of attention. *Prep* is learning to read what the ground already is. *Set* is the small
  irreversible act of commitment (a cutting pushed in). *Forget* is the hardest and the point:
  learning to let a living thing do its own work on its own clock, and to trust that the withering
  stalk, the closing canopy, the fallen leaf are the system working, not failing.
- **The smallest possible version.** Tam's line (copy #18): the chili by the kitchen door is enough
  to start — "not because a pepper feeds anybody, but because you walk past it every day and one
  afternoon you pick one and put it in the pot, and something has changed in the relationship that no
  amount of reading does." Praxis begins at the smallest scale that still crosses the line from
  reading to relationship.
- **Recognition as a practice.** Hōʻiʻo (#42), the wild fern already growing in the wet ditch, is
  food you did not plant — "waiting only to be recognised. The succession got there first." A core
  praxis is *seeing what is already there* before adding anything.

## II. The outer techniques — the repeatable acts

Cross-cutting methods that recur across many plants; the per-plant specifics live in [raw/](raw)
and the copy.

- **The living cutting.** The signature PSF act: a branch or stem pushed into the ground *becomes a
  plant*. Gliricidia (#11, a whole branch → a fence that grows), moringa (#19), cassava (#5), chaya
  (#21, dried first so the cut seals), kō (#40, a joint laid on its side), the two spinaches
  (#23–24). Technique varies (woody vs. green, dry-first vs. straight-in) but the gesture — and the
  lesson that life propagates itself cheaply — is one.
- **Chop-and-drop (the transaction).** Cut a nitrogen-fixer or pioneer and leave everything you cut
  on the ground: leaf becomes mulch, roots shed nitrogen, the neighbour eats both *at the same
  moment*. Pigeon pea (#6), ice cream bean (#10), gliricidia, kukui (#39). The cut is not tidying;
  it is feeding.
- **Pruning to keep food in reach.** Many plants go to the sky if left (moringa to 25 ft, aibika to
  16, chaya, katuk). Cutting them low is not restraint of the plant but service to the harvest —
  keep the food where the hands are.
- **Reading the harvest signal from the plant, not the calendar.** The withering ginger/turmeric
  stalk, the yellowing yam vine, the wrinkling-and-dropping lilikoʻi, the milky-vs-watery ʻulu sap,
  the greening acerola losing vitamin C as it reddens. The plant keeps the clock; praxis is learning
  to read it.
- **The hand the forest still wants.** Against the fantasy that a food forest runs itself: cherimoya
  (#17) and vanilla (#53) *require* hand-pollination on the plant's schedule. Some abundance is only
  released by a person's daily attention. Naming this honestly is itself praxis — it sets true
  expectations.

## III. Scaling the practice — from door to guild

- Start at the door (the chili, a spinach, the fern already there). Add one layer at a time as the
  attention and the shade both deepen. Let the succession (nurse before understory, canopy before
  the plants that want its shade) set the pace — the order is derived, per [succession.md](succession.md),
  not forced.
- The weight-bearing horizon (from [psf-project]): if the tool becomes real for many people, praxis
  is what has to travel — not the plant list (that is bounded and shippable) but the *way of standing*
  toward the ground. This document is where that is kept teachable.
